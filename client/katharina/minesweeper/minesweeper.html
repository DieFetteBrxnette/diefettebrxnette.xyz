First up: Minesweeper Basics
Your goal is to open up all numbers on the board, but be careful not to hit a bomb or you will lose.
Every number you open up helps you not to hit a bomb, because the number stands for the amount of bombs near its field.
For example: The number of a field is 3, so there are three bombs within the eight fields around it. Note: You cannot lose with your first turn, so it doesn't matter which field you open up at first. If you still don't understand the game, read Wikipedia's explanation or join our Discord

How to play it in discord using Katharina: The board generates as usual, but you have to select a field by writing it's coordinate in chat.
A coordinate consists out of the column and the row of the field. A valid field starts with the letter and ends with the number, if there's a space between these two doesn't matter.
You will see, that your field is valid, because Katharina reacts to it with upto four reactions.
A blue, red and yellow field and a red x.
If you react with the
- blue field, you will open up the field and may get a new number or lose the game because you open up a bomb. You have to risk it, otherwise you won't be able to win.
- red field, you mark the field as a bomb, this is just a reminder for you and does not affect the game
- yellow field, you mark the field for later, if you're unsure about the field being a bomb or a number.
- red x, you undo the selection and can select a new field.
If you find any sort of typo or bug or just don't understand anything, feel free in our Discord.
